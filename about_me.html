Hi! Here's some information about myself

Professional background

I am computational researcher with a background in data visualization, algorithm development, and machine learning applications.

I have a M.Sc. degree and a B.E. degree from Stony Brook University. While at Stony Brook I did research computational materials science research at the nearby National Synchrotron Light Source II (NSLS-II), and completed a thesis on X-ray Data Processing & Visualization Techniques for Analysis of Functional Materials.

After finishing my thesis I moved to Germany for a year where I worked as a Research Assistant (Wissenschaftlicher Mitarbeiter) at the University of Stuttgart's Computational Imaging Systems (CIS) department. My research focused on applying deep learning models to artifact correction in undersampled 3D computed tomography (CT) images.

Though I was offered to extend my stay in Germany, I decided to move back to the U.S. at the end of my research contract. Since moving back I have been actively searching for roles where I can continue using my skills to advance scientific research, including positions in data science, scientific computing, and research software engineering.

Personal/Misc. information

I was born and raised in the Buffalo, New York area, and am currently living there again since I left Germany.
In my free time I enjoy working on personal coding projects including physics simulations and game development.
